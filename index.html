What keeps me up at night

  1. Do emotions have a lifespan? Why do some emotions last longer while others last for a split second?
  2. Will there ever be a perfect debate? Perfect in terms of having absolute consensus upon definitions.
  3. Will there ever be an absolute, independent thinker who has all their thoughts generated by themselves?
  4. How do you defeat a man who knows everything, at any time?
  5. Will there ever be a scenario in which all of mankind stands on the same side of an argument?
  6. Is there a threshold to the level of impact that forces us to begin weighing the pros and cons?
  7. Is it possible to explain a very challenging academic topic without using the terms and logic of that field? If so, would anything be lost?
  8. Is there a threshold that separates what is truly humanly impossible from what is extremely challenging but still achievable?

Animal names I use to plan the sh*t out of everything

I started experimenting with making plans and time management since grade 8 (or maybe 9 — I can't remember). I wrote a book about the planning techniques I used to survive high school, which nobody cared about or read.

I realized that the most important aspect of a good plan is to be flexible and multidirectional. A good plan should incorporate things that you want and don't want to do, but it should be so flexible that you are not restricted. The plan should not be guiding you. You should be guiding the plan.

Elephant
Beaver
Owl
Dolphin
Bookworm
Bees

I tend to use these animal categories with the following rules:

  1. I should be completing at least 3 animals a day.
  2. I should be prioritizing these animals at the start of each day so I have a general sense of what's more pressing, but rule 1 still applies.
  3. Some animals may take longer than the rest, and that's fine.
  4. Some tasks may fall under multiple animals. That's fine — completing that task counts towards completing multiple animals.
  5. If a task does not fall under any of these categories, it is not a task.
